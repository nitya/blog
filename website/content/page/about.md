## 🙋🏽‍♀️ Why "Connecting Dots"?

A unifying theme across for my work is that I like to **connect the dots** between complex technologies and real world experiences - in ways that **help people learn** critical skills. For instance, I've helped people learn:
 - to embrace public speaking (GDCFPday, MakeTheTalkHappen)
 - to prioritize self-care (Sorry Not Sorry)
 - to visualize tech concepts (SketchTheDocs)
 - to build tech community (Google Developer Groups)
 - to organize tech events (DevFest NYC, IWD NYC, Study Camps)
 - to educate & mentor students (Adjunct Faculty, SUNY New Paltz)
 - to build innovative solutions (Distinguished Member of Tech Staff, Motorola Labs)
 - to build strategic programs (Cloud + AI, Developer Relations PM @Microsoft)
 - to build scalable content (Cloud + AI, Cloud Advocate @Microsoft)

Two decades in tech have shown me that there are very few _completely new_ ideas. Rather, we build on learnings from the past, or revisit old ideas with increased success because the ecosystem is now ready to embrace them at scale. _By constantly looking for familiar patterns and connecting the dots_ we can find ourselves more resilient to changes, and more receptive to the growth opportunities that change invariably brings.
